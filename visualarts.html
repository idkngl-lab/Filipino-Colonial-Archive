Discover the paintings, sculptures, churches, and artistic traditions that shaped Filipino culture during over 300 years of Spanish rule.

History

During the Spanish colonial period (1565–1898), art in the Philippines was strongly influenced by religion. Churches commissioned paintings, wood carvings, sculptures of saints (santos), and grand architectural designs. Filipino artists combined Spanish artistic traditions with local craftsmanship, creating a unique cultural identity that can still be seen today.
